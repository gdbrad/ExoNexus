# Notes on GEVP Operator Basis Splitting and Time-Reversal Symmetry

**Reference:** [arXiv:1803.09673](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1803.09673)

When performing a Generalized Eigenvalue Problem (GEVP) analysis in Lattice QCD, mixing operators with different spatial structures (e.g., standard quark bilinears vs. covariant derivatives) can lead to catastrophic numerical instabilities in standard solvers like `scipy.linalg.eigh`. This applies universally across all flavor sectors, including light mesons ($u/d$), charmonium ($\bar{c}c$), and mixed states like D-mesons ($\bar{c}u$).

This document summarizes the mathematical root of the problem and the physical justification for dynamically splitting the operator bases.

## The Problem: Indefinite Correlation Matrices
The standard GEVP requires solving:
$$ C(t) v_n(t, t_0) = \lambda_n(t, t_0) C(t_0) v_n(t, t_0) $$

Standard solvers rely on Cholesky decomposition, which imposes a strict mathematical requirement: **the metric matrix $C(t_0)$ must be strictly positive-definite**. All of its eigenvalues must be $> 0$.

When building a large mixed operator basis (e.g., $5 \times 5$), we often encounter a `LinAlgError` regarding the "leading minor not being positive definite." This happens because the diagonal of $C(t_0)$ contains mixed signs (some positive, some negative), rendering the entire matrix mathematically indefinite.

## Physical Origin: $\cosh$ vs $\sinh$ Time Dependency
As noted in Section 3 of the referenced paper, operators possess intrinsic Time-Reversal ($T$) or Parity symmetries determined by their gamma matrix structures. In your codebase (`meson_factory.py`), the temporal gamma matrix is denoted as **$\gamma_4$**.

On a periodic lattice, the correlation functions wrap around the temporal boundary:

1. **Symmetric Correlators (e.g., $P \to P$ or $V \to V$ without $\gamma_4$):**
   These consist of strictly identical source and sink operators. Because their $T$-parity is symmetric, their temporal correlation functions asymptotically fall off as strictly positive **$\cosh$** functions:
   $$ C(t) \propto e^{-E t} + e^{-E(L_t - t)} \rightarrow \cosh\left[E\left(\frac{T}{2} - t\right)\right] $$
   Because $\cosh > 0$, the diagonal components of the matrix are safely positive.

2. **Odd/Mixed Correlators (Operators containing $\gamma_4$):**
   When operators include the temporal gamma matrix $\gamma_4$, they acquire odd relative time symmetry. The backward-propagating thermal state acquires a minus sign, and the correlator falls off as a **$\sinh$** function:
   $$ C(t) \propto e^{-E t} - e^{-E(L_t - t)} \rightarrow \sinh\left[E\left(\frac{T}{2} - t\right)\right] $$
   In Euclidean time, $\sinh$ crosses zero in the middle of the lattice and carries negative diagonal elements depending on the operator's intrinsic phase. 

If a $\cosh$-like operator and a $\sinh$-like operator are placed into the **same** GEVP matrix, the diagonals will conflict in phase. The GEVP solver expects a single coherent metric space, but receives an indefinite mixed-metric space.

## Operator Separation by Irrep
To solve this, we must separate operators based on their Time-Reversal ($T$) parity. Purely spatial derivatives (e.g., `none`, `nabla`, standard double-derivative `D`, and chromomagnetic `B`) do not flip the $T$-parity of the boundary state. **The Even/Odd parity is strictly determined by the base gamma operator.**

Based on the interpolators defined in `meson_factory.py` (`a0`, `pi`, `pi_2`, `rho`, `a1`), the operator bases decompose as follows:

* **Pseudoscalar ($A_{1u}$ or $0^{-+}$)**
  * **Even/Cosh:** `pi` ($\bar{q}\gamma_5 q'$) + spatial derivatives (`_none`, `_nabla`, `_B`, `_D`)
  * **Odd/Sinh:** `pi_2` ($\bar{q}\gamma_5\gamma_4 q'$) + spatial derivatives (`_none`, `_nabla`, `_B`, `_D`)

* **Vector ($T_{1u}$ or $1^{--}$)**
  * **Even/Cosh:** `rho` ($\bar{q}\gamma_i q'$) + spatial derivatives (`_none`, `_nabla`, `_B`, `_D`)
  * **Odd/Sinh:** Temporally mixed tensors (e.g., if a `rho_2` mapped to $\bar{q}\gamma_i\gamma_4 q'$ is added).

* **Scalar ($A_{1g}$ or $0^{++}$)**
  * **Even/Cosh:** `a0` ($\bar{q} \mathbf{1} q'$) + spatial derivatives (`_none`, `_nabla`, `_B`, `_D`)
  * **Odd/Sinh:** Pure temporal vectors (e.g., $\bar{q}\gamma_4 q'$).

* **Axial Vector ($T_{1g}$ or $1^{++}$)**
  * **Even/Cosh:** `a1` ($\bar{q}\gamma_i\gamma_5 q'$) + spatial derivatives (`_none`, `_nabla`, `_B`, `_D`)
  * **Odd/Sinh:** Temporal mixed axial tensors. 

## Code Implementation
In `analysis/analyze_spec.py`, these are dynamically filtered via string matching against the operator names generated by `meson_factory.py`. Any operator name containing your temporal tags (like `pi_2`, `gamma4`, `g4`) will be isolated into a mathematically healthy `odd_sinh` block, multiplied by $-1$, and fed into its own independent GEVP solver. 

By isolating the operators into physically orthogonal $T$-parity sub-blocks, the standard solvers will perfectly factorize them, and the ground states of both symmetry sectors can be cleanly extracted.
